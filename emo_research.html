If you are a potential employer, I don't mention this project on my resume because it isn't physics. This is my attempt at emo music research and performing data science on a non-standard problem.

In 2026, Washington University in St. Louis, Missouri, hosted a first-of-its-kind academic conference on emo. This was thanks to the efforts of Varun Chandrasekhar (Washington University) and Patrick Mitchell (University of Cincinnati). I interviewed them for Swim Into the Sound, which you can read here.

I submitted an abstract to the conference and attained a poster slot. I made a set of slides to share if there happened to be an opportunity to present.

In the next week, there will be a link at the end of this page to csv files of the combined dataset, and copies of the individual datasets, which you may use (it's all public info anyway). Information about attribution and citations are included at the bottom of this page.

The code will be public in the next month or so. Please check back if you are interested in that.

For all inquiries, please get in contact via email at emodatascience@gmail.com.
